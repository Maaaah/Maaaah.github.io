The X-Prize Tricorder


The X-Prize Medical Tricorder Project


Part of founding team working on the Qualcomm Tricorder XPrize. Bringing early diagnosis to the home, and providing medical access in areas without the doctors. We were architects for hardware design, developed and troubleshooted a wearable Electrocardiograph, Photoplethysmograph and Accelerometer sensor array. Built a wireless camera otoscope and spirotoscope. We provided software development for microcontrollers, embedded Linux and Android. Along with typical engineering we provided algorithms for filtering and adaptive measurement of biomedical signals and image processing.

  • System Architecture
  • Hardware Digital and Analog Design
  • Embedded Software and DSP Algorithm Development
  • Android Mobile Development
  • AWS Cloud Services

Assistant for the Blind


Helping the Blind Navigate the visual world:


A wearable mobility device for blind or visually impaired people that utilizes multiple cameras and image recognition to help them navigate their immediate vicinity. It does the seeing for people who have a hard time recognizing exit or bathroom signs and does user interaction through text to speech and automatic speech recognition. We did the Android BSP and Linux kernel development, hardware debugging and FDA documentation.

  • Hardware debugging and Acoustic Design
  • Linux/Android BSP Development
  • Android Application Development

Hearing Aids


Helping the Hard of Hearing Navigate the acoustic world:


Hearing loss is a massive problem for the aging population. Not being able to communicate effectively drastically reduces the Quality of Life of people with hearing loss. We have spent years beginning at personal and market requirements, understanding the problem and synthesizing theoretical information into a coherent signal processing strategy. We done the complete chain for Algorithm design, starting with models in Matlab or Simulink, then implemented in assembly on various DSP platforms. We have extensive laboratory experience, including bring-up, automation, complete electro-acoustic testing and trials with human listeners. We did documentation for the various international standard bodies.

  • Algorithm Simulation and Design
  • DSP implementation
  • Automated test development

Tablet Speaker Phone


Centralized stations in each conference room can be expensive, and products do not target the prevalence of telecommuting and distributed work. We designed a central communications hub based on a 3 microphone, consumer grade tablet for smart conference rooms. We provided beamforming, noise reduction and interactive mixture models that adaptively located and tracked speakers and noise sources torival the tethered microphone sound quality. We built the Android application on top of kernel drivers that utilized a front end DSP for algorithm implementation.

  • Algorithm Simulation and Design
  • DSP implementation
  • Linux/Android BSP development
  • Automated test development

Audio Digital Signal Processing:


We have brought many audio concepts to life including DSP in headphones, Frictionless UI with Automatic Speech Recognition and Text to Speech for IoT home automation and audio direction of arrival and distance estimation. We provide 20 years of experience coming up with, developing and testing audio algorithms. We've helped out with mechanical design, analog electronics, electro-acoustic and human testing. We can implement ideas across any hardware vectors and form-factors.

  • Algorithm Simulation and Design
  • Fixed Point DSP implementation
  • Bare Metal implementation
  • Human test development, MOS, MUSHRA

Internet of Things, Home Automation:


Right now the Internet of Things for home automation is exploding. Also exploding is the security concerns and how you always need a smartphone. We designed a system for home automation through voice commands, seemlessly built into light switches. We developed Automatic Speech Recognition on the embedded Cortex-M4 processors for security; no voice to web, or hackers listening in. It was secured by a proprietary ad-hoc, in house network only, no uPnP backdoors. We developed the hardware design to allow micro-metering of power used through switch, and an Android Application.

  • Hardware design
  • Fixed Point DSP implementation
  • Audio algorithms (beamforming, noise reduction, low power always-on, machine learning ASR)
  • Embedded Firmware
